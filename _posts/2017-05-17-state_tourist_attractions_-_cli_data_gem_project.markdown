I love to travel. I don't do it as often as I would like to, but every year I try to visit at least one new country and one new state. The first question a person asks when planning a trip is what am I going to see there. Some people will go for the less visited, more special places, while others won't leave New York City without taking a picture on Times Square. That is why I have decided to build an app that would list the most popular tourist attractions in each State - that could be the starting point of each trip.

I have spent more time than I expected on this project. First of all, I couldn't come up with an idea - started a couple of gems and deleted them half way through because I wasn't passionate about them. Then, finally, came up with something I would like to use myself. Sure, there are many apps about tourism and travel destinations, that is why there is room for improvement - I could have such features as photos and more listings per state with links to their official pages.

The easiest part of the project was watching Avi's video 😂, the second easiest thing was building the CLI class. Avi was right when he said that the most intimidating thing for a coder was a blank page. It took me a day just to overcome the anxiety of starting something completely new. Bundler definitely made things easier, since it created the structure, and from there, I could continue my work. One of the most difficult parts was scraping the website that featured the article I was using for my project. I would get some undesirable text and spacing was off because the web data wasn't as well organized as in the pages I had scraped for the previous lessons.

There were a couple of things I did for the first time, ever. Like, recording a video and it posting it to YouTube. I believe many people hate listening to their own voice, but, as a person with an accent, I hate it double! I re-recorded the walkthrough until I was hoarse because I didn't like the ways I pronounced words. At least, I learned how to use QuickTime Player!

I finished my project today and will be waiting for review and watching more videos 🎬 .
