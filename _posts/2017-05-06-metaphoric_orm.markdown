Let's just say coding doesn't seize to surprise me! Before starting these course, I have never thought that, while programming, I needed to think in real-life terms in order to write an eloquent code. 

Currently, I am working on my final Ruby projects and have just completed the Music Library CLI lab. ORM is hard because I need to be mindful of the relations between various objects. In this lab, I needed to connect Genre, Song, and Artist classes in the way they related to one another in real life. For instance, a genre has many songs and it is connected to an artist through these songs. It is a difficult notion, but very much relatable to real life. In one of the video lectures, Avi drew a framework of how these classes related to each other, and that was the most helpful thing for me because it helped me see the lines of connections. 

We can notice ORMs all the time. The Flatiron school has many students from different countries, and it is connected to these countries through its students. While each student lives in a particular country and takes the Web Development course at Flatiron.

I've often made fun of my mom because she uses mathematical terms in real life, pretty soon I'll start thinking in programming terms, too! :)
