title:  "**Why I decided to learn how to code**"
date:   2017-03-15 17:38:59 +0000
---


Although I have been thinking of learning how to code for a while now, it took me a while to get to it. I've always seen myself as a humanitarian, a liberal arts major student. I love languages, cultures, psychology... I love how, depending on where we come from, we think and behave differently. I guess that is why computers have always scared me - this phenomenon stems from my cultural upbringing. You see, there are "feminine" and "masculine" professions. The tech field is definitely masculine, that is why it was always easier for me to get my male cousins do all the "computer stuff" for me. 

It started from my childhood and continued into my professional life. While doing multiple internships in digital marketing, I couldn't help but feel that I didn't know enough. I worked with blogs, newsletters, and social media platforms, but there was always only that much I could do. I had to rely on someone who had technical skills I didn't possess. I wanted to become more independent, which would lead to self-empowerment. I was still hesitant about whether I could do it or not, that is why it took me a year to apply. At some point, I started thinking of learning how to code as learning a new language. This is the language that connects people from all over the world. I am already fluent in three, so how hard can it be?! Well, it turned out, harder than I expected! :) 

I am exactly three weeks into the online program, and I am enjoying it! It is an emotional experience, but definitely a rewarding one. It is frustrating when my code doesn't pass, but when it does, I feel like a genius! And that is why I want to learn how to code.
