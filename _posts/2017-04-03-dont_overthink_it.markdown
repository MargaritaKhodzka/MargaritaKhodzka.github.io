Well, there are a few subtle but important things to understand about OO Tic Tac Toe and coding in general, for that matter.

Regardless of what the instructors and my online "classmates" were saying, I just kept hard coding values. Thus, overthinking it. General terms are more applicable, so I learned from my mistakes. Speaking of mistakes, learn from them! Error messages are the most important sources of information.

Another mistake I kept making was the number of arguments (or lack of thereof) for methods and variables, especially while interpolating. So, pay attention to that and don't forget that "board" is an @object!
